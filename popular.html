Coconut Noodles

Ohn No Khao Sw√®, aka coconut noodles, is a Burmese delight of thin noodles in a creamy coconut soup. Topped with chicken, eggs, and spices, it's a visual and gastronomic feast that's perfect for breakfast or a light meal.

Coconut noodles have been a staple in Burmese cuisine for centuries, with variations found across different regions of the country. The use of coconut milk in the soup is believed to have originated from Indian and Thai influences.

Coconut noodles are a rich source of carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats. However, due to the use of coconut milk and oil, they can also be high in calories and saturated fat. It's important to enjoy them in moderation as part of a balanced diet.

Mohinga

  • Description
  • History
  • Nutrition

Mohinga is a popular Burmese dish made with rice noodles in a flavorful fish broth, topped with various ingredients such as boiled eggs, crispy fritters, and herbs. It's often considered the national dish of Myanmar and is enjoyed throughout the country.

Mohinga has a long and rich history in Myanmar, dating back to the ancient Pyu Kingdom. Over time, it became a popular dish among the Burmese people, with different regions adding their own unique twists and ingredients to the recipe.

Mohinga is a nutritious and filling dish, with protein from the fish and eggs, carbohydrates from the noodles, and various vitamins and minerals from the herbs and spices. However, it can also be high in sodium due to the fish broth, so it's important to enjoy it in moderation as part of a balanced diet.
